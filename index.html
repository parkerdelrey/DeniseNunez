Bronx Physician Calls for Cultural Sensitivity

By Parker Herren, 6 November 2020

Dr. Denise Nuñez, a Bronx physician, is passionate about providing health services that are accessible and culturally sensitive for Spanish-speakers.

"They're illegal, they're hard workers, they're really nice people," said Dr. Nuñez. "So, for very low cost, we actually have them come here and they can get their physicals done and checked out."

According to a Pew Research Center study, the greater New York metropolitan area had the largest population of undocumented immigrants in the U.S. in 2016.

"They need more doctors that look like [them] - more culturally sensitive doctors," said Dr. Nuñez. "They get very nervous and they think we're going to call the cops on them.”

According to the CDC, COVID-19 hospitalizations for Hispanic or Latino persons are 4.6 times higher than their white counterparts. "The numbers say it," said Dr. Nuñez. "I saw it, I lived it because I was in the ICU all the time."
